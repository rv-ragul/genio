Logo Design: Clash of Logos

Just a Boring Description!

Graphic design is a profession, academic discipline and applied art whose activity consists in projecting visual communications intended to transmit specific messages to social groups, with specific objectives. Graphic design is an interdisciplinary branch of design and of the fine arts.

Its practice involves creativity, innovation and lateral thinking using manual or digital tools, where it is usual to use text and graphics to communicate visually. The role of the graphic designer in the communication process is that of encoder or interpreter of the message. They work on the interpretation, ordering, and presentation of visual messages.

General Event Rules

    Participation:
  1. Teams of up to 2 members are allowed, with each team submitting one entry.
  2. Only one team member can use a PC or laptop.
    Competition Format:
  1. The competition consists of two rounds. The first round is the logo design round, lasting 45 minutes.
  2. The First round, participants are provided with two businesses or themes to create logos for.
  3. The second round is the logo recreation round, lasting 15 minutes, where participants must recreate logos for five famous brands.
    Selection Process:
  1. The best logo from the first round and performance in the second round will be considered.
    Competition Duration:
  1. The logo design round lasts for 45 minutes, and the logo recreation round is 15 minutes.
    Submission:
  1. Logos should be in .png format, with a brief explanation of the design.
    Scoring:
  1. Logo design contributes 80% to the score, while the design explanation adds 20%. The second round performance is also considered.
    Tool Usage:
  1. No AI tools allowed, but you can use inspirational resources like Dribbble.
    Jury Decision:
  1. The jury's decision is final.
    Plagiarism Check:
  1. Plagiarism will result in score disqualification.
