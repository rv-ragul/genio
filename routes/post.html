Just a Boring Description!

At most meetings or conferences, when you present a poster presentation, it means that you will display your poster and be available for viewers to ask questions about your research and how it is represented on your poster. Like an oral presentation delivered with a slideshow, there are many ways to design and present your research on a poster. There are also, however, a few important considerations and conventions to be familiar with.

General Event Rules

  1. Teams can consist of maximum of 3 members and minimum 1.
  2. Only one poster will be allowed to present per team.
  3. Names and Institute name should appear on the bottom corner of the poster.
  4. Names and Institute name should appear on the bottom corner of the poster.
  5. All lettering and diagrams should be large enough to be read from a minimum of 2 feet.
  6. The official language for the poster will be English.
  7. Every team/participant will have 45minutes to complete the task and 15minutes for explaination.
  8. Organize the content logically, including sections for introduction, methodology, results, discussion, and conclusions.
  9. Use concise text, bullet points, and visuals (such as graphs, charts, and images) to convey information effectively.
  10. If participant violates any above mention rules will be disqualified from the event.
  11. Any query regarding the poster presentation event, kindly contact the co-ordinators.
